How can I recycle?

Along with having their household trash picked up for disposal, Mount Desert Island area residents have several options when it comes to reducing what gets thrown out via recycling.

Tremont

The only place available for recycling in Tremont are the metal containers in the lower parking lot of the town office. There are two hoppers there each for plastics, tin cans, papers, and cardboard, respectively. The containers are clearly labeled. It is important for Tremont residents to note that these Dumpsters are solely for recycling.

Southwest Harbor

Southwest Harbor residents can do all of their recycling at EMR, located at the transfer station on Long Pond Road. At EMR, they are asked to separate their items into three categories before recycling them. The three categories are newspaper, cardboard, and containers (1 gallon or less).

Bar Harbor

According to www.barharbormaine.gov,  the town “takes a proactive stance on recycling.” The Bar Harbor Solid Waste and Recycling center accepts white goods, metals, and recyclables such as glass, plastic, paper, tin cans and cardboard to be recycled at the town transfer station.

Mount Desert

In Mount Desert, there is a recycling drop-off center on Sargent Drive that can be used to recycle all traditional materials like metal, glass, plastic, cardboard and paper.

Trenton

For Trenton residents, there are metal containers located in the town office parking lot for each of the following: cardboard, paper, plastic containers and tin cans, and glass jars. The town has a contract with EMR so an alternative option for anyone from Trenton is to take their materials directly to EMR’s facility in Southwest Harbor.

Cardboard Pick Up

Because it is mandatory to recycle cardboard on Mount Desert Island, Gott's is contracted by island towns to pick up residential cardboard. There is no fee to residents. The schedule is as follows:
